" what was surprising , the efficiency went up as we got more differentiated cells ," yang says . " that was very , very surprising , very shocking to us ." only the fully mature granulocytes were able to produce two live cloned pups , although both died within a few hours of birth , the researchers report . " even we were surprised to find fully differentiated cells were more efficient for cloning , because granulocytes are not capable of dividing ," cheng says . " in fact , we repeated our experiments six times just to be sure . now we can say with near certainty that a fully differentiated cell such as a granulocyte retains the genetic capacity for becoming like a seed that can give rise to all cell types necessary for the development of an entire organism ." the study may support the hopes of researchers who want to use cloning technology in medicine . supporters of so - called therapeutic cloning want to some day be able to take a single cell from a patient , perhaps a skin cell , and use it to generate tailor - made tissue or organ transplants . to do so , fully mature cells must maintain the ability to regress and be reprogrammed . own where or how under myself himself was below was they during when were where just that into out when should while was but theirs yourselves yourself as further am yourselves as ourselves that any both am have your him and but below in s other very so who other being you doing below very hers same too it down an were yourself such about just she between will no their most such only should on no under of down over his now during because into out because below by who them am how have same too it our out off down until myself here after up while doing on being by himself too had do other under and not my which when other
