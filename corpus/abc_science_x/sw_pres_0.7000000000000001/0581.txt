" what was surprising , the efficiency went up as we got more differentiated cells ," yang says . " that was very , very surprising , very shocking to us ." only the fully mature granulocytes were able to produce two live cloned pups , although both died within a few hours of birth , the researchers report . " even we were surprised to find fully differentiated cells were more efficient for cloning , because granulocytes are not capable of dividing ," cheng says . " in fact , we repeated our experiments six times just to be sure . now we can say with near certainty that a fully differentiated cell such as a granulocyte retains the genetic capacity for becoming like a seed that can give rise to all cell types necessary for the development of an entire organism ." the study may support the hopes of researchers who want to use cloning technology in medicine . supporters of so - called therapeutic cloning want to some day be able to take a single cell from a patient , perhaps a skin cell , and use it to generate tailor - made tissue or organ transplants .
